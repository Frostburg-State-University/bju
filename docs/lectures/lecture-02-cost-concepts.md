## Learning Objectives

1. Distinguish **fixed vs. variable**, **direct vs. indirect**, and **recurring vs. non-recurring** costs in software projects.
2. Apply the concepts of **opportunity cost**, **sunk cost**, and **marginal cost** to engineering decisions.
3. Construct a **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** view across the software lifecycle.
4. Identify modern cost categories (cloud, API, token, GPU) that did not exist in classical SEE.

## Topics

- Cost taxonomy: fixed/variable, direct/indirect, recurring/non-recurring, capital/operating.
- Behavioral cost concepts: opportunity, sunk, marginal, average.
- Lifecycle cost: acquisition, development, operation, maintenance, retirement.
- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) — what classical models miss.
- A TCO worksheet for a modern SaaS or AI-assisted system.

## In-Class Exercise

You are evaluating a small internal tool. List every cost category that should appear in its five-year TCO, including any AI-era costs (LLM tokens, vector DB, GPU). Compare lists in pairs.
