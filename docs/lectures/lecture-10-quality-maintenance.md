# Lecture 10 — Quality & Maintenance Economics

!!! info "Session Info"
    **Date:** Friday, 2026-06-12 &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Time:** 16:20–18:10 &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Room:** SY109
    **Instructor:** Dr. Zhijiang Chen

## Learning Objectives

1. Quantify the **cost of poor quality** along the lifecycle.
2. Plot the **defect-cost curve** (cost-to-fix vs. discovery phase).
3. Evaluate the **ROI of quality investments** (review, testing, monitoring).
4. Model **technical debt** as a financial liability.
5. Decide between **maintain, re-engineer, and rewrite** strategies.

## Topics

- Boehm's defect-cost curve.
- SQUARE and other quality cost frameworks.
- Reliability investment ROI.
- Software maintenance cost share of TCO (often 60–80%).
- Technical debt: principal vs. interest; "stop-the-bleeding" interventions.
- The rewrite decision: when is it economically rational?

## In-Class Exercise

Given a defect distribution across phases, compute the present-value cost saving from shifting 30% of defects "left" by one phase.

## Homework

- Build a technical-debt ledger for one module of your project. Estimate principal, interest rate, and payback period for fixing it.
